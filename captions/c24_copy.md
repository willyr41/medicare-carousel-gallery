## Caption
**Carousel: $13 Billion Medicare Advantage Boost**

CMS just finalized a massive 2.48% payment increase for Medicare Advantage plans in 2027, funneling over $13 billion in new funds to private insurers. This is a huge reversal from the near-flat 0.09% increase proposed back in January.

When factoring in risk score trends like population changes and coding practices, the real increase is closer to 4.98%. CMS also decided to stick with the 2024 risk adjustment model instead of updating it, giving insurers more time to adjust. However, they are cracking down on some practices, like banning audio-only diagnoses from being used to calculate risk scores.

With 35 million seniors relying on Medicare Advantage, the big question is: will this $13 billion windfall translate into better benefits and lower costs for patients, or just higher profits for insurers?
