BREAKING: For the first time EVER, Medicare Advantage insurers must publicly reveal how often they deny your care.

Starting March 31, 2026, new CMS rules force total transparency on prior authorization — the process insurers use to approve or deny medical treatments before you receive them.

Here's what the data already shows:

→ 4.1 million prior auth requests were denied in 2024 alone
→ That's 7.7% of ALL medical requests rejected
→ Over 80% of appeals were ultimately OVERTURNED
→ Doctors spend 13 hours per week fighting insurance paperwork

Now insurers must post approval rates, denial rates, and decision turnaround times online for everyone to see.

New deadlines are also in effect: 7 days for standard decisions, 72 hours for urgent care (down from 14 days).
