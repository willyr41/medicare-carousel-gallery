## Caption
**Carousel: Elevance Health Faces MA Sanctions**

CMS has granted Elevance Health a two-month extension, pushing the deadline for potential Medicare Advantage enrollment sanctions to May 30, 2026. The insurer is facing severe penalties over what CMS calls "substantial and persistent noncompliance" with risk adjustment data reporting.

The agency alleges that for seven years (2018-2025), Elevance submitted data corrections for unsupported diagnosis codes using encrypted USB flash drives instead of the required electronic systems. CMS also claims the company continued certifying the accuracy of its data despite knowing unverified codes hadn't been properly corrected.

Elevance, which has 1.9 million Medicare Advantage members, is pushing back. Their CFO called the situation a "policy and payments dispute" rather than a data submission issue, arguing it's about how retroactive corrections should be handled. While some specific plans have been exempted, the threat of an enrollment freeze still looms over the insurance giant.
