## Caption
**Carousel: $18.6 Billion Star Ratings Windfall**

CMS just finalized a major overhaul of the Medicare Advantage Star Ratings system, a move expected to funnel an extra $18.6 billion to private insurers over the next decade.

The agency is removing 11 administrative measures from the ratings and eliminating the Health Equity Index reward, which was designed to incentivize care for underserved populations. Instead, CMS will continue rewarding consistently high performance across all measures. These changes mean more insurers will likely earn higher ratings and qualify for lucrative government bonus payments.

While CMS is adding a new depression screening measure for 2027, they are also rolling back regulations, including removing restrictions on how and when agents can talk to beneficiaries. Most shockingly, CMS is reportedly considering a policy to automatically enroll seniors into Medicare Advantage plans rather than original Medicare.
