## Carousel: $18.6 Billion Star Ratings Windfall

CMS just finalized sweeping changes to the Medicare Advantage Star Ratings system — and insurers are celebrating. The agency is eliminating 11 quality measures and scrapping the Health Equity Index entirely, a move analysts estimate will funnel $18.6 billion more to insurers over the next decade.

The overhaul means more plans will qualify for bonus payments, even as the bar for quality accountability drops. CMS is also removing restrictions on agent conversations with beneficiaries and even considering auto-enrolling seniors into Medicare Advantage plans. Critics argue this prioritizes insurer profits over patient outcomes, while supporters say it reduces regulatory burden and expands access.
